This chapter presents a set of representative tests of the main types of calculations and models, comparing results with different sources found in the literature.

## 4.1 Critical Point Calculations

Peng and Robinson (*15*)(references.md#ref-15) reported results for critical point calculations of different mixtures. Tables 4.1–4.2 compare results with errors generally under 5%. The algorithm used is based on Heidemann et al. (*16*)(references.md#ref-16) with numerical derivatives as described by Stockfleth et al. (*10*)(references.md#ref-10).

## 4.2 Adiabatic Flash Calculations

Tables 4.3–4.4 present an adiabatic flash for a four-component mixture (benzene, cyclohexane, methylcyclohexane, n-hexane) using Peng-Robinson. Results are consistent with Da Silva and Báez (*4*)(references.md#ref-4).

## 4.3 Bubble Point Pressure

Using the van Laar model for the liquid phase and ideal gas for the vapor phase, bubble points were calculated for a methanol(1)/water(2) binary mixture at 298K. Parameters from Orbey and Sandler (*21*)(references.md#ref-21). Table 4.6 shows errors averaging less than 1%.

## 4.4 Dew Point Calculations

For the 2-propanol(1)/water(2) system using the Wilson model, Tables 4.7–4.8 compare results with Smith et al. (*22*)(references.md#ref-22) and Da Silva and Báez (*4*)(references.md#ref-4).

## 4.5 Bubble Point Temperature

A four-component mixture using Raoult's law. Table 4.9 shows results are absolutely consistent with Da Silva and Báez (*4*)(references.md#ref-4).

## 4.6 Isothermal Flash Calculations

An equimolar mixture of n-heptane(1) and butane(2) at 300K, 100kPa using RKS. Table 4.10 shows agreement to the third decimal place with Da Silva and Báez (*4*)(references.md#ref-4).

## 4.7 Binary Interaction Parameter Calculation

For the CO2(1)/butane(2) system at 357.57K. Table 4.11 lists experimental data (Da Silva and Báez (*4*)(references.md#ref-4)). Table 4.12 shows kij = 0.1357, consistent with Sandler (*3*)(references.md#ref-3) (0.135) and Da Silva and Báez (*4*)(references.md#ref-4) (0.1359).
